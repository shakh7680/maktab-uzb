President Schools

The Presidential School is a specialized public educational institution whose activities are aimed at identifying and educating gifted children to train highly qualified specialists.

14 Presidential schools will be established in accordance with the Resolution of the President of the Republic of Uzbekistan dated February 20, 2019 PP-4199 "On measures to establish presidential schools" in the Republic of Karakalpakstan, regions and the city of Tashkent.

In 2019, Presidential boarding schools were opened in Tashkent, Khiva, Nukus and Namangan.

The initial limited number of presidential schools is due to the need to test a completely new model for the management of these institutions. Presidential schools are planned to be built in all regions of Uzbekistan from 2020 to 2021.

Free general secondary education is provided in presidential schools.

The main purpose of the Presidential schools is to identify and educate gifted children through the use of advanced technologies in the educational process, as well as to support and encourage gifted youth, to enrich the spiritually rich and ensuring guarantees of upbringing of intellectually developed generation.

The main tasks and priorities of the Presidential School:

  • to identify, select and educate gifted children, create conditions for their full development, as well as reveal the intellectual, scientific and creative potential of students;

  • to organize in-depth study of natural and exact sciences, mastering foreign languages, engineering and information technologies, to create the necessary conditions for the development of innovative ideas and developments of students;

  • to establish modern methods of teaching and assessing students' knowledge, as well as an interdisciplinary approach to teaching based on the integration of educational programs, and then apply the positive experience to the public education system of the republic;

  • to direct students to the profession, to develop their leadership skills and public speaking skills, to improve their skills in critical thinking, information retrieval, analysis and processing, to apply the acquired knowledge in practice;

  • to ensure the proper participation of students in international Olympiads, competitions and contests in general education subjects;

  • to form in students a sense of patriotism and love for the Fatherland, tolerance, respect for laws, national and universal values, strong beliefs and views on life;

  • to establish cooperation with national and foreign general secondary and higher education institutions in the same field to ensure the continuity of curricula, to continue the education of graduates in advanced foreign universities and their branches in the country.

Public Schools

In Uzbekistan, secondary education is divided into two stages. The first stage includes nine years of compulsory schooling with the same programs all over Uzbekistan. The second stage covers education and vocational training after nine years. It includes general secondary education and specialized secondary education. Young people receive general secondary education while staying in school for the tenth and eleventh grades. Upon successful completion, they get a Certificate of Complete Secondary Education

In Uzbekistan 11 years of education are compulsory and free, beginning with 4 years at primary school, and followed by 2 phases of secondary education taking 5 and 2 years respectively. Primary school begins at age 6 and there is no specific leaving examination after the 4 years are complete.

The next 5 years are spent at general secondary school from ages 10 to 15. Following that, there is a choice of between 2 to 3 years of upper education at either general or technical vocational schools. The former provides a certificate of completed secondary education and the opportunity to enter university, the latter a diploma of specialized secondary education, through a network of secondary vocational institutions.
